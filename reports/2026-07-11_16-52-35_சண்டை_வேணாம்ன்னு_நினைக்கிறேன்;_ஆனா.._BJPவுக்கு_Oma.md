In the constitutional framework of Indian UTs with legislatures (such as Delhi and Puducherry), the Lieutenant Governor (appointed by the President of India on the advice of the Union Government) retains significant executive control over key administrative areas like public order, police, and land. Omar Abdullah’s statement reflects the structural friction inherent in this governance model, framing the constitutional division of powers as a deliberate political strategy by the BJP to bypass local democratic mandates. While the legal division of power is factual, the assertion of malicious intent to render the elections meaningless is a highly contested political narrative central to the regional parties' campaign against the Union Government.
