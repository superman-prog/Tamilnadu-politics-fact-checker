## 🚨 FLAGGED CONTESTED CLAIMS (SUMMARY)

* **Minister R. Sakkarapani**: "We are planning to set up integrated checkposts at state and district borders, combining the RTO, Civil Supplies CID, and Transport departments in a single building to conduct unified checks and completely eliminate ration rice smuggling."
  * **Verdict**: **MISLEADING**
  * **Analysis**: While the minister outlines a structural plan to integrate departments at border checkposts, the claim of "completely eliminating" smuggling is highly contested. The smuggling of free Public Distribution System (PDS) ration rice from Tamil Nadu to neighboring Kerala and Karnataka is a systemic, multi-million rupee illicit trade. Opposition parties (AIADMK and BJP) frequently document and protest the continuous leakage of PDS rice, alleging systemic collusion between local officials, checkpost personnel, and smugglers. Setting up integrated checkposts is a administrative step, but historically, checkpost-level corruption has bypassed such structural integrations.

* **Minister R. Sakkarapani**: "We will discuss with the Chief Minister, consult all departments, gather public feedback, and definitely distribute coconut oil through ration shops in the future if needed."
  * **Verdict**: **PURE OPINION / POLITICAL PROMISE**
  * **Analysis**: The demand to distribute coconut oil instead of imported palm oil through the PDS is a highly sensitive political issue in Tamil Nadu, particularly in the western "Kongu" belt (a major coconut-producing region). While the minister frames this as a pending decision under consultation, agricultural unions argue that the DMK government has repeatedly delayed this transition due to the powerful palm oil import lobby, making this statement a tactical political reassurance rather than an active policy implementation.
