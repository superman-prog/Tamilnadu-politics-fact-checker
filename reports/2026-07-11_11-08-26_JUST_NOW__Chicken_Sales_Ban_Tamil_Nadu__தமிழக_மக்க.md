## 🚨 FLAGGED CONTESTED CLAIMS (SUMMARY)

* **Tamil Nadu Poultry Traders Association**: "Feeding chickens within 15 hours of sale artificially inflates their weight, causes environmental degradation, and increases the risk of infectious disease transmission to the public."
  * **Verdict**: **MISLEADING**
  * **Analysis**: The core of this dispute is economic rather than ecological or epidemiological. Poultry traders are protesting against poultry farm owners who feed chickens immediately before transport and sale (a practice known as "crop stuffing" or "wet feeding"). This practice artificially inflates the bird's weight with undigested feed, forcing retail traders to pay higher wholesale prices for weight that is ultimately discarded during slaughter. While the traders' association frames this as an environmental and public health hazard to gain regulatory traction and public sympathy, the primary driver is a commercial dispute over profit margins and weight measurement standards between retail traders and wholesale poultry producers in Tamil Nadu.
