## 🚨 FLAGGED CONTESTED CLAIMS (SUMMARY)

* **TASMAC Union Representative / Advocate**: "The DMK government is directly violating its election promise to regularize temporary and contract workers who have completed five years of service."
  * **Verdict**: **TRUE / CONTESTED**
  * **Analysis**: During the 2021 Tamil Nadu Assembly Election campaign, the DMK's manifesto (specifically Promise #311) committed to regularizing contract, temporary, and consolidated-pay workers in various government departments and state-run corporations after a certain period of service. TASMAC employees, who have worked on a contract/consolidated basis for up to 20 years, argue this promise has been completely ignored, leading to widespread protests. The state government, however, cites financial constraints and the unique administrative structure of TASMAC to delay regularization.

* **TASMAC Union Representative / Advocate**: "The 25% salary hike announced by the Minister is a deceptive figure because, after mandatory deductions, workers only receive a take-home pay of around ₹16,000, which is impossible to survive on in Tamil Nadu."
  * **Verdict**: **MISLEADING**
  * **Analysis**: While the math regarding net take-home pay after PF, ESI, and other deductions may be accurate for entry-to-mid-level shop employees, framing the 25% hike as entirely deceptive is an exaggeration. The state government implemented the hike to appease workers, but the union argues that a percentage-based hike on a low base salary does not solve the fundamental issue of inflation and the lack of a formal government pay scale.

* **TASMAC Union Representative / Advocate**: "The government does not provide maintenance funds for TASMAC shops, forcing workers to collect an extra ₹10 per bottle from the public to cover operational costs, only for the government to then brand the workers as corrupt."
  * **Verdict**: **CONTESTED**
  * **Analysis**: The "extra ₹10 per bottle" issue is a highly controversial political topic in Tamil Nadu. The opposition parties (AIADMK, BJP, NTK) frequently allege that this extra collection goes directly to DMK-affiliated syndicates and politicians. The union representative here attempts to shift the blame, claiming that lack of government funding for basic shop expenses (rent, electricity, transport, breakage) forces employees to overcharge customers. The government officially maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward overcharging and has suspended numerous employees for the practice.
